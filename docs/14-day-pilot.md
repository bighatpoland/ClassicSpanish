# 14-Day Pilot

## Goal

Validate that the recommended method is sustainable and improves access to spoken Spanish under real mobile conditions.

The pilot is short on purpose. It is meant to answer:

- Can you actually keep this up on an iPhone?
- Does the method improve speaking access, not just recognition?
- Does the quiet/voice split work in real life?
- Is the `SRS` cap realistic?

## Daily template

Target: `25-35 minutes`, `6 days/week`

### Core block

- `8 min` SRS review
- `7 min` easy input with transcript
- `10 min` spoken recall or AI voice turns
- `0-5 min` micro-fix for one form or pronunciation issue

### Minimum viable day

Use this on bad or busy days:

- `5 min` SRS review
- `5 min` easy input
- `5 min` one short spoken answer recorded in Voice Memos

The minimum viable day prevents zero-days without demanding full energy.

## Suggested prompts for spoken output

Cycle through these:

- introduce yourself
- describe your day
- say what you like and dislike
- explain weekend plans
- compare today with yesterday
- give a simple opinion
- retell a short dialogue or clip
- answer "what did you do?", "what do you want?", "why?", "with whom?", "when?"

## Rules for the pilot

1. No more than `5` new cards on any day.
2. No new cards at all if review backlog starts feeling stressful.
3. Input must be easy enough that you can catch the main meaning.
4. Spoken output can be slow and broken; completion matters more than elegance.
5. Grammar help is allowed only after a real speaking problem appears.
6. Missed day recovery should be gentle:
   return to the normal cap instead of "catching up" with a double session.

## Tracking

Track these numbers once per day:

- total minutes
- did you do quiet mode, voice mode, or both
- number of review cards completed
- number of new cards added
- did you record or speak aloud: yes/no
- stress level from `1-5`
- speaking confidence from `1-5`

## Evaluation at day 1, 7, and 14

Record one `60-90 second` spoken sample on the same topic family:

- who you are
- what your week looks like
- what you like doing
- one simple opinion

Judge the sample using these questions:

1. How often did you freeze because you had no accessible words?
2. How many simple connectors did you use:
   `y`, `pero`, `porque`, `tambien`, `entonces`
3. Could you produce several complete simple sentences?
4. Did you sound more understandable than before?
5. Did your answer come out faster, even if still imperfect?

## Success criteria

The method passes the pilot if, by day 14:

- you completed at least `10` of the `14` planned days
- `SRS` stayed within the time cap most days
- you produced spoken Spanish on at least `8` days
- backlog stayed manageable
- the day 14 sample is easier to produce than the day 1 sample
- the method feels realistic enough to continue for another month

## Failure signals

Adjust the system before continuing if any of these happen:

- review backlog becomes the dominant experience
- input is so hard that it produces mostly frustration
- speaking feels so stressful that you consistently avoid it
- grammar fixes expand into long study sessions
- the workflow feels too fragmented for real phone use

## Next decision after the pilot

After day 14, choose one of three paths:

1. `Keep the method, keep the tool stack`
   if consistency and speaking progress are both solid
2. `Keep the method, simplify the tools`
   if the pedagogy works but the phone workflow is clumsy
3. `Keep the core method, add more speaking support`
   if you are consistent but still avoiding live output and want one weekly conversation
